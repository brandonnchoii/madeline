I am a storyteller.

When I was 9 years old, my parents bought me my first CVS flip video camera to document the happenings of our summer vacations at the National Parks. When I was 11 I was entrusted with a Sanyo camcorder that my sister and I used to bring to life the scripts we had written, inspired by our favorite TV shows like The Office, Summer Heights High, and Flight of the Conchords. When I reached high school and my interest in stories and filmmaking was clearly turning from a mere hobby into a true passion I graduated to the realm of DSRL. From there I began experimenting with a whole new world of visual storytelling.

Currently a senior at Duke University, I designed my own major in storytelling and have continued to experiment with and study its different forms. I enjoy stories that offer a new perspective, digging deeper into things we think we already know. I am drawn to stories that illuminate the ways that we are all connected as human beings. Over the past year, I interned with the Bitter Southerner, an online, cultural, and literary magazine about the South, spent a semester in Los Angeles exploring all that Hollywood has to offer, and interned at ArtsATL, a non-profit that promotes a vibrant arts community in Atlanta. Most recently, I have discovered a passion for audio storytelling and podcasting after attending Werk It 2016, a womenâ€™s podcasting conference, at WNYC studios in New York City.

I believe in the power of stories to connect us, to inspire us, and to allow us to understand one another a little bit better.
